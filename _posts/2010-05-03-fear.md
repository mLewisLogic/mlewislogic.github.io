&ldquo;Entrepreneurism is glamorized and romanticized, but at the end of the day, it&rsquo;s really, really hard work, and it requires a lot of sacrifice&mdash;not just from you, but from the people around you. Make sure your friends and family are ready to support you, and then jump in. Don&rsquo;t let the fear keep you from doing it. You only live once, so if this is what you&rsquo;re passionate about, don&rsquo;t have any regrets looking back on your life. Just do it.&rdquo; -<http://techcrunch.com/2010/05/01/advice-from-founders-who-bootstrapped-their-way-to-success-2/>

&nbsp;

It&rsquo;s amazing how subtle the fear is. Pretty much what [Seth Godin][1] refers to as the &ldquo;lizard brain.&rdquo; I catch it myself all the time whenever the thought &ldquo;Hrmmm, I&rsquo;m way too busy right now, I&rsquo;ll deal with this later.&rdquo; Whenever I find myself thinking that I realize that I&rsquo;m nowhere near my personal limits, and the fear of taking the risk of making progress is what holds me back. I feel that only the most successful people suppress that fear and allow themselves to operate as quickly as the world will permit. Going with the flow is easy, but I feel that it would cause me to look back on my life with regret if I let it become my defacto response.
